# Paper Outline — AgentDiff: Capability-Gated Dichotomy

**Working title:** *The Capability-Gated Dichotomy: When (and Why) LLM Agents Are Robust to Surface but Not Semantic Perturbations*

**Target venue:** NeurIPS 2026 main / ICLR 2026 / ARR–EMNLP findings (decide after experiment 2 finishes).

**Length:** 9 pages + references.

---

## 1. Abstract (≤ 250 words)

> Large language model (LLM) agents are widely assumed to be robust to surface-level paraphrasing of their inputs while remaining vulnerable to semantic perturbations of key entities or operators. We provide the first cross-model evidence that this dichotomy is **not universal but capability-gated**: it emerges only when the agent can already solve the task. Across 25 (model × benchmark × agent-type) cells spanning six LLMs (1 B–671 B parameters, including the closed-source MiMo-v2.5-pro), three benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, HotpotQA) and two agent scaffolds (CoT, ReAct), the difference Δ between semantic and surface inconsistency rate scales linearly with the agent's task accuracy (Pearson r = +0.44, p = 0.020; OLS slope = +18.3 pp / accuracy unit, 95 % CI [+2.8, +33.8]). When task accuracy ≥ 0.65 the dichotomy holds in **8 / 8 cells** with mean Δ = +14.6 pp (Fisher exact p = 0.003); when accuracy < 0.65 the dichotomy collapses to mean Δ +1.2 pp with only 6 / 17 cells positive. We further document a complementary mechanism-level dichotomy: even when overall inconsistency rates match, semantic perturbations diverge from the original trajectory ~0.11 reasoning steps earlier (paired t = −2.30, p = 0.021) and are 3 × less likely to be self-corrected (0.8 % vs 2.6 %, p = 0.005, χ² test on 5-pattern distribution: p = 0.042). Our results reveal that *robustness to surface noise is itself an emergent capability* and provide a falsifiable accuracy-threshold criterion for predicting where agent robustness assumptions hold.

## 2. Introduction (~1 page)

**Hook.** A common engineering assumption in LLM-agent design is that prompt formatting, sentence reordering, and irrelevant injections do not affect outcomes, while semantic edits do. This assumption underlies CheckList-style robustness suites and justifies skipping spell-checkers or context-pruners.

**Gap.** No large-scale, multi-model, multi-task study has tested whether this dichotomy is universal. Existing work studies one model, one benchmark, one scaffold, or aggregates across heterogeneous setups so the dichotomy hides as an average.

**Contributions:**
1. A cross-model controlled study (25 cells, 6 LLMs, 3 benchmarks, 2 scaffolds, n=20–50/cell) with identical perturbation operators, judge, and variant generator.
2. **The capability-gated dichotomy result.** Δ_IR scales linearly with task accuracy; the dichotomy holds robustly only when acc ≥ 0.65 (8/8 cells, Fisher p = 0.003).
3. **A mechanism-level secondary signature.** Independently of Δ_IR, semantic perturbations diverge ~0.1 step earlier and are 3× less likely to be self-corrected.

---

## 3. Related Work (~½ page)

- **Behavioral robustness for LLMs.** CheckList, Stress-test, NLI invariance, contrast sets — single-model, no agent loop.
- **Agent benchmarks.** ToolBench, AgentBench — measure raw success, not perturbation invariance.
- **Emergent capabilities.** Wei et al., BIG-Bench — discontinuous capability scaling at certain compute thresholds. Our finding aligns: surface robustness is itself an emergent capability.
- **Reasoning-chain perturbation.** Self-Refine, ReAct — examine reasoning errors but not invariance.

## 4. AgentDiff Framework (~1 page)

### 4.1 Five perturbation operators (Table 1)
- **Semantic** (require LLM judge of equivalence):
  - paraphrase — full reformulation preserving meaning
  - synonym — verb / adjective swaps
- **Surface** (deterministic, structural-only):
  - reorder — clause permutation
  - format — bullet-list / line-break re-rendering
  - distractor — explicit "irrelevant note" prefix injection

### 4.2 Variant generation pipeline
Variants generated by a fixed-aligned `qwen2.5:3b` for all cells; equivalence verified by an LLM judge (same model) for semantic; structural-token preservation check for surface (numbers, units, wh-words, "?" must survive).

### 4.3 Inconsistency rate (IR) and Δ
$$\mathrm{IR}_{\text{type}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbb{1}[\text{answer}(x_i^{\text{type}}) \neq \text{answer}(x_i)]$$
$$\Delta = \mathrm{IR}_{\text{semantic}} - \mathrm{IR}_{\text{surface}}.$$

### 4.4 Propagation pattern
For each variant we record divergence step, cascade depth, and one of {consistent, self_correct, late_diverge, early_diverge, cascade}.

## 6. Results

### 6.1 Capability-gated dichotomy (Figure 1, Table 2)
> **Headline.** Δ scales linearly with task accuracy (Pearson r = +0.44, Spearman ρ = +0.40, OLS slope = +18.3 pp/acc, 95% CI [+2.8, +33.8]). At T = 0.65, all 8 capable cells show Δ > 0 with mean +14.6 pp; the 17 incapable cells average +1.2 pp with 6 positive (Fisher exact p = 0.003).

## 7. Discussion (~½ page)

- *Why does dichotomy require capability?* Hypothesis: only sufficiently capable models build a meaning-preserving abstraction that ignores surface form; weaker models latch onto surface tokens.
- Connection to emergent capabilities literature; analogue of "chain-of-thought emerges with scale".
- Practical implication: **don't trust surface robustness on a model unless it has ≥0.65 accuracy on the task.**

---

## 8. Limitations (mandatory honesty)

- 25 cells; Group A is 8 cells — modest; n=50 expansion in flight.
- Variants generated by a single 3B model, judge by same family.
- IR uses exact-string match for math, LLM judge for HotpotQA — different noise.
- "Capability tier" labelling correlates with parameter count but is not deterministic.
- No mechanistic (hidden-state) experiments; phenomenological only.

---

## 9. Conclusion (3–4 sentences)

> Surface-vs-semantic robustness in LLM agents is conditional on capability. Below an accuracy threshold of ~0.65 there is no measurable dichotomy; above it the gap is reliably +14.6 pp. This makes "robustness to surface noise" a probe of capability, not an assumption. Future work: scale to 50–100 cells; test mechanistic hypothesis with attention/hidden-state probes.
